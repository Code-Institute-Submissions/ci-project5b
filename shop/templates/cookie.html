Cookie Policy

What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on a user's computer by websites they visit. These files contain data such as user preferences, session information, and other browsing-related details. Cookies serve various purposes, including improving website functionality, personalizing user experiences, and tracking user behavior.

Types of Cookies:
  1. Session Cookies: These cookies are temporary and are deleted once the user closes their web browser. They are used to maintain session information and enable essential website functions.
  2. Persistent Cookies: Persistent cookies remain on the user's device even after the browser is closed. They store user preferences and login information, allowing websites to remember users across sessions.
  3. First-Party Cookies: First-party cookies are set by the website domain the user is visiting. They are commonly used for website functionality, analytics, and customization.
  4. Third-Party Cookies: Third-party cookies are set by domains other than the one the user is visiting. They are often used for advertising, tracking user behavior across websites, and retargeting.
Cookie Usage:
  • Authentication: Cookies are used for user authentication, allowing websites to recognize logged-in users and provide personalized content.
  • Personalization: Cookies store user preferences such as language settings, font size, and customized layouts, enhancing the browsing experience.
  • Analytics: Cookies track user behavior and website interactions, providing valuable insights for website owners to optimize content and improve usability.
  • Advertising: Cookies are used for targeted advertising, enabling advertisers to deliver relevant ads based on users' interests and browsing history.
Cookie Management:

Users can manage cookies through their web browser settings. Most browsers allow users to view, delete, and control cookie preferences, including accepting or blocking cookies from specific websites.

In summary, cookies play a crucial role in enhancing website functionality, personalizing user experiences, and enabling various online services. However, it's essential to balance their benefits with user privacy concerns and adhere to relevant data protection regulations.
